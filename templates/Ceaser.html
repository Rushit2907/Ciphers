What is Additive Cipher?

Additive Cipher is also reffered as Ceaser cipher or Shift cipher. Additive/Ceaser/Shift cipher needs Plaintext and One Key.

To convert plaintext into ciphertext we just have to add character and key and take modulus by 26. for example, if we have key '3' and plaintext character 'A' then the ciphertext character will be 0(A)+3(key)%26 = 3(C).

En(X)=(X+key) mod 26

To convert ciphertext into plaintext we just have to substraction character and key and take modulus by 26. for example, if we have key '3' and plaintext character 'C' then the ciphertext character will be 3(C)-3(key)%26 = 0(A).

En(X)=(X-key) mod 26
