What is Multiplicative Cipher?

Multiplicative cipher needs Plaintext/Ciphertext and One Key.

To convert plaintext into ciphertext we just have to multiply character and key and take modulus by 26. for example, if we have key '3' and plaintext character 'B' then the ciphertext character will be 1(B)*3(key)%26 = 3(C).

En(X)=(X*key) mod 26

To convert ciphertext into plaintext we have to find multiplicative inverse of key using extended euclidean algo and then multiply it with character and take modulus by 26. for example, if we have key '3' and cipher character 'C'.

first must check key is Co-Prime to the 26.
Quotient R1 R2 Reminder t1 t2 t = t1-(Q*t2)
8 26(Z26) 3(key) 2 0(initial) 1(initial) (0-(8*1))=-8
1 3 (R2 from above Row) 2 (R from above Row) 1 1 (T2 from above Row) -8 (T from above Row) 9
2 2 1 0 -8 9 -26
Multiplicative Inverse of key = 9 mod 26 = 9

Now we multiply the key inverse and ciphertext character (9(key inverse) * 3(C)) mod 26 = 1(B) .

En(X)=(X*key_inverse) mod 26
